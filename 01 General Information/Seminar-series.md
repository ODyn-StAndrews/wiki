# Seminar series

# University of St Andrews

The [Centre for Ethics, Philosophy, and Public Affairs](https://ceppa.wp.st-andrews.ac.uk/) (CEPPA) host regular seminars, often relevant to environmental and climate ethics. Request to be added to the mailing list by email to the CEPPA director: [ceppadirector@st-andrews.ac.uk](<mailto:ceppadirector@st-andrews.ac.uk>).

# Virtual

The [US Climate Variability (CLIVAR)](https://usclivar.org/) program hosts webinars on a wide range of climate-relevant topics. See further details [here](https://usclivar.org/webinars).

The [Paleoclimate Model Intercomparison Project (PMIP)](https://pmip.lsce.ipsl.fr/) hosts a monthly webinar, titled PMIP-WINGS (Web-based INteractive Global Seminars). See past talks and sign up for alerts [here](https://pmip4.lsce.ipsl.fr/doku.php/wings:index).

The [Biogeochemical Argo](https://biogeochemical-argo.org/) community hosts a weekly seminar on all things related to ocean biogeochemistry, with a particular leaning towards observational work. You can view the seminar calendar [here](https://calendar.google.com/calendar/u/2?cid=Y19mYjloNTFoOHNqZHBoZjJnMXJ0MXVhaTRqMEBncm91cC5jYWxlbmRhci5nb29nbGUuY29t), and you can get notifications through the BGC-Argo listserv (email Roberta Hotinski at [hotinski@princeton.edu](mailto:hotinski@princeton.edu) to be added). The seminar is recorded and you can request recordings of previous meetings. 

The American Physical Society has a [Topical Group on the Physics of Climate](https://engage.aps.org/gpc/home), and they host a monthly online seminar. They are usually announced on the GFD mailing list. Links to past recordings, and a schedule of future seminars, can be found [here](https://engage.aps.org/gpc/resources/seminar-series).
